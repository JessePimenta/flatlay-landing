The Four Fundamentals of a Flat Lay

1. Keep a Clean and Neutral Background

You want to make sure that the surface you lay items on does not take away from the products. Using a consistent color, whether it is a white tabletop or sheet, makes the image simple.

Tip- Use a marble counter or polished wooden top. It is a great way to add dimension and interesting dynamic to your shot.

@happilygrey
@chris.b.a

2. Find the Sunlight

Using bright, natural light is a great way to showcase the colors of the items you’ve selected. Try to avoid dim lighting unless intended, no filter or app will be able to fix unwanted shadows.

Tip - Refrain from using flash on your camera. Ideally you want bright, evenly consistent lighting so using lamps with super white bulbs will do wonders. Keep in mind that less is more with ample light; simple objectives will pop on camera.

@juliahengel

3. Themes and Color Schemes

A great way to tie your shot together is to invoke inspiration and a tone by using palettes of colors. Pairing a color set to a themed shot draws emotions and reactions from audiences.

Tip- Pairing accessories into an image are a great way to add a dimension of individuality. Using a vintage leather bag or a flower in the shot adds a new dimension that builds character and adds a story. Leveraging contrasting colors in the shot can add pop and flair, further drawing a viewer in.

@fashfitness
@hellomissmay

4. Spacing and Angles

Be sure to shift around items to get your shot. Fill the white space with clothes and distance your items into interesting arrangements. Another technique is to have products bleed off the edge of your post.

Tip – Use various textures. Items such as leather, denim, tweed or silk together and with accessories will help tie your look together. The best part of a Flatlay is that it allows others to imagine themselves in the product. Use this to your advantage to show others how an outfit, survival pack or product might work for them.

@chrisellelim




The 5 Best Apps for a Flat Lay

Flatlay (Coming Soon)

We help you do it the best. You use any image you have already taken on Instagram, Pinterest or within your library and plug it alongside a collection of products you have curated. Share it with the Flatlay community and the world via your page and social channels.

Instagram

Instagram (iOS / Android) – IG is an incredible community to share and build a following around your content. You have the ability to tag brands, people AND @Flatlayapp to be featured. Includes tools such as filters and overlays to help you further tweak your images before you post.



Snapseed (iOS / Android)

Snapseed – This is a great retouching and photo editing tool that helps enhance your photo to give you the cleanest image. There are great filters and effects that will help you make the most of your shots.



Lightroom (iOS / Android)

A professional photo editor created by Adobe. Incredibly agile and responsive interface that can help adjust hues, color tones and effects while also providing photo-shoot preset settings.



Bazaart (iOS)

This app easily helps you make really clean backdrops to crop your product shots against a flat white background. You will want to make sure to take your shots on a smooth white backdrop. Crop everything out of the image except for the products then hit “create”. You can then make a collage of your products that you can take and post wherever you would like to share it.
